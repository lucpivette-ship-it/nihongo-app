# JLPT Roadmap — N3 -> N2 -> N1 (2 years)

Goal: pass JLPT N3, then N2, then N1, within two years of 2026-07-22.

| Kanji grade (elementary) | Rough JLPT equivalent |
|---|---|
| Grade 1 (80 kanji) | ~N5 |
| Grade 2 (160 kanji) | ~N5-N4 |
| Grade 3 (200 kanji) | ~N4 |
| Grade 4 (200 kanji) | ~N4-N3 |
| Grade 5 (185 kanji) | ~N3 |
| Grade 6 (181 kanji) | ~N3-N2 |
| Beyond kyoiku kanji (jōyō) | ~N2-N1 |

This mapping is approximate — JLPT levels test vocabulary, grammar, and reading/listening ability, not just kanji count. Use this as a rough sequencing guide, not a strict curriculum.
